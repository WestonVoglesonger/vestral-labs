Business Model

How we work with the industry

Four revenue streams that align our success with the success of the BCI ecosystem. We make money when the industry moves faster.

Stream 1

Developer Platform Licensing

CORTEX Pro: Full access to the benchmarking framework for internal development. Run unlimited benchmarks, integrate with your CI/CD, get detailed analytics.

  • Unlimited benchmark runs on your hardware

  • Private artifact storage and analytics

  • Custom kernel harness development support

  • Priority access to new benchmark suites

Stream 2

Enterprise Validation & Benchmarking

Paid evaluation runs and performance audits for teams that need third-party validation. Useful for regulatory submissions, investor due diligence, and partnership discussions.

  • Independent performance evaluation

  • Regulatory-ready documentation packages

  • Cross-platform comparison reports

  • Expert consultation on performance optimization

Use cases

Companies use enterprise validation when they need credible third-party numbers:

  • • FDA submission support documentation
  • • Series A/B investor technical due diligence
  • • Partnership technical evaluation
  • • Competitive benchmarking studies

Stream 3

Certification & Standards Programs

Formal certification that a device or algorithm meets defined standards. Creates market signal for quality and accelerates trust between vendors and customers.

Why certification matters

In mature industries, certification is table stakes. "CORTEX Certified" becomes shorthand for "this meets the bar"—reducing evaluation burden for everyone.

Phase 3+

Certification Programs

Certification becomes meaningful only after we've established benchmarking credibility and the industry recognizes our standards. This is a Phase 3 priority.

  • • Safety Envelope Compliance
  • • Interoperability Certification
  • • Performance Tier Badges

Stream 4

Incubation / Venture Studio

Some infrastructure we build will have standalone value. When we identify high-upside internal projects, we may spin them out as separate entities— capturing equity upside while staying focused on core infrastructure.

This isn't our primary business model. It's an option that becomes available when we're deep in the problem space and see opportunities others don't.

  • Internal R&D with commercialization potential

  • Spin-out equity participation

  • Deep domain expertise from infrastructure work

The logic

Building infrastructure means understanding the problem space deeply. Sometimes the best solution isn't more infrastructure—it's a product. When that happens, we want the option to pursue it.

Project Shelob is the most obvious candidate: a distributed coordination layer that could become its own company once the ecosystem is ready.
